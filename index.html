I'm Madeleine

I am a student at the University of Toronto studying Computer Science. To me, coding is about creativity and problem-solving. It allows people to bring imaginative ideas to life in a form that others can experience and interact with, making it a unique form of creative expression.

My favourite projects are those that challenge my abilities while also solving real-world issues to directly uplift the communities we live in. I believe technology should be used to bridge gaps in access, support vulnerable populations, and drive meaningful change.

📌 Here are some things I've been a part of:

Software Developer - Full Stack @ CGEN (Feb. 2024 - Present)

The Aqualens project, an initiative by the Centre for Global Engineering at UofT, is developing an image-based water quality assessment tool that enables practitioners and community members to conduct water quality tests directly in the field. Delivered as a smartphone app, the tool facilitates efficient communication between field practitioners and end-users, supporting data-driven policy decisions.

As a full-stack developer on this app, I have contributed to the development of the Flutter components, including the home and results screens, as well as the Flask API, database, and computer vision pipeline. Since July 2024, I have also served as the project lead, overseeing communication with the research team, managing the project timeline, and assigning deliverables to a team of ten developers.

In March 2025, the team pitched the app’s MVP.

Executive @ UTQC (Feb. 2024 - Present)

As an executive for UofT's Quantum Comptuing student group, I lead recruitment and marketing efforts for the club’s inaugural Quantum Computing Magazine, working with the team to define project timelines. I oversee the submission and review process, ensuring articles align with the magazine’s editorial standards and contribute to a cohesive, high-quality publication.

Beyond UofT, I work with UBC’s Quantum Computing Club to support and promote undergraduate-led publications in the quantum computing field.
